If we have measurements that are tightly correlated, then they carry the same information and we can discard one or more of the values without losing information. On a correlation matrix, a correlation of 1 indicates that the values or variables are self-similar, i.e. the same, where a correlation of -1 indicates that the variables are opposites. A correation score of 0 indicates that there is no relationship between the variables. For our sample data set, there are strong correlations between these measurements. Here is another figure that shows the correlations between two measurements. We also add the measurement of the vehicles' noise levels.
